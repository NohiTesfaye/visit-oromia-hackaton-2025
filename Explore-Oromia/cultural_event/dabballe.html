Dabballee

Sacred Childhood in Oromo Culture

The First Stage of Life

Dabballee (ages 0-8) are Oromo children in their first Gada cycle, celebrated as sacred beings who connect the community to Waaq (God) through their purity.

Rituals & Traditions

Naming Ceremony

Children are named on the 8th day, blessed with butter and honey.

Sacred Protection

Wear amulets (qallu) and avoid haircuts to preserve spiritual energy.

Oral Education

Learn Oromo values through stories, songs, and proverbs from elders.

Life as Dabballee

"Children are the eyes of Waaq"

Dabballee enjoy special privileges: they don’t labor, can enter any home for food, and are seen as peacemakers during conflicts.
