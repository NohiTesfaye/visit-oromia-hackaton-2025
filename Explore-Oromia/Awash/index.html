Discover Awash National Park

Established in 1966, Awash National Park is Ethiopia's oldest national park, located in the Great Rift Valley just 225 kilometers east of Addis Ababa. The park covers 756 square kilometers of acacia woodland and grassland, bisected by the dramatic Awash River which cascades over volcanic rocks to create stunning waterfalls.

Awash Falls

The spectacular Awash Falls mark where the river plunges into a gorge, creating a breathtaking natural wonder.

Fantale Volcano

The dormant Fantale volcano dominates the park's landscape with its perfect conical shape.

Hot Springs

Relax in the park's natural hot springs, known for their therapeutic properties.

Wildlife of Awash

Awash National Park is home to an incredible diversity of wildlife, including several species that are rare or endangered. The park's varied habitats support different animal communities, from the riverine forests to the open savannah.

Oryx

The park's signature animal, with its long straight horns and striking facial markings.

Greater Kudu

This majestic antelope with spiral horns is commonly seen in the park's woodlands.

Hamadryas Baboon

Troops of these distinctive baboons are frequently encountered near the river.

Birdlife

Over 450 species including the rare Somali ostrich and many raptors.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Awash National Park depends on what you want to experience:

Dry Season (October - March)

  • Best for wildlife viewing as animals congregate near water sources
  • Pleasant daytime temperatures (25-30°C)
  • Clear skies and minimal rainfall
  • Ideal for photography with good visibility

Wet Season (April - September)

  • Lush green landscapes and flowing waterfalls
  • Birdwatching is excellent with migratory species present
  • Fewer visitors means more exclusive experience
  • Some roads may be difficult to navigate
Pro Tip: Early mornings and late afternoons are the best times for game viewing throughout the year, as animals are most active during these cooler hours.

Location & How to Get There

Awash National Park is conveniently located along the Addis Ababa-Djibouti highway, making it easily accessible for visitors.

By Road

  • From Addis Ababa: 3-4 hour drive (225km) via the Addis-Djibouti highway
  • From Dire Dawa: 5-6 hour drive (300km)
  • Well-maintained roads with clear signage
  • Several car rental companies offer 4x4 vehicles with drivers

By Air

  • Nearest airport is in Awash town (20km from park entrance)
  • Charter flights available from Addis Ababa
  • Regular domestic flights to nearby cities (Dire Dawa, Harar)
  • Park transfers can be arranged from airports
