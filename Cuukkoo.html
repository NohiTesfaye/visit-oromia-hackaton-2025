Cuukkoo

Oromo's Celebratory Honey Wine

About Cuukkoo

Cuukkoo is a traditional Oromo honey wine, fermented for weeks with gesho (hops-like herb). Slightly sweet and effervescent, it’s reserved for special occasions like weddings and harvest festivals.

Making Cuukkoo

Ingredients

  • Raw honey
  • Water
  • Gesho leaves
  • Yeast (optional)

Fermentation Process

1
Mix Honey & Water

Combine in a clay pot (1:3 honey-to-water ratio).

2
Add Gesho

Stir in gesho leaves for bitterness and preservation.

3
Ferment

Store in a cool place for 2-4 weeks, stirring weekly.

The Drink of Celebration

Cuukkoo is poured from a special vessel (Dhadhi) during ceremonies. Sharing it signifies trust and friendship in Oromo culture.
