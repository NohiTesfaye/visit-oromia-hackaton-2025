Ancootee

Oromo's Wholesome Barley Pancakes

About Ancootee

Ancootee are thick, savory barley pancakes, often served with spiced butter or honey. A staple breakfast in rural Oromo households, symbolizing simplicity and sustenance.

Hearty

High Fiber

Ancient Recipe

Making Ancootee

Ingredients

  • Barley flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Spiced butter

Traditional Method

1
Mix Batter

Combine barley flour, water, and salt into a thick batter.

2
Cook Slowly

Pour onto a hot clay griddle, cook until golden brown.

3
Serve Warm

Drizzle with spiced butter or honey.

A Farmer's Breakfast

Ancootee is traditionally eaten by Oromo farmers at dawn, providing energy for a day’s work. Its simplicity reflects the Oromo value of resourcefulness.
