About Buna Qala

Buna Qala are roasted barley or wheat grains, lightly salted and served during Oromo coffee ceremonies. Crunchy and flavorful, they balance the bitterness of coffee while symbolizing hospitality.

How to Prepare Buna Qala

Ingredients

  • Whole barley/wheat
  • Salt
  • Clay pot (optional)

Traditional Method

1
Roast Grains

Dry-roast grains in a clay pot until they pop and turn golden brown.

2
Season Lightly

Sprinkle salt while still warm for even coating.

3
Serve with Coffee

Present in small woven baskets during coffee ceremonies.

A Symbol of Togetherness

Buna Qala is inseparable from Oromo coffee ceremonies, representing the bond between host and guest. The act of sharing grains and coffee fosters community and dialogue.
