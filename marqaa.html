Marqaa

Oromo's Wholesome Barley Porridge

About Marqaa

Marqaa is a nutritious barley porridge, slow-cooked to perfection. It’s a breakfast staple in Oromo households, often sweetened with honey or enriched with spiced butter. Known for its creamy texture and energizing properties.

Comfort Food

High Fiber

Slow-Cooked

Marqaa Recipe

Ingredients

  • Barley flour
  • Water or milk
  • Honey (optional)
  • Spiced butter

Preparation Steps

1
Mix Flour & Water

Combine barley flour with cold water to form a smooth paste.

2
Slow Cook

Simmer on low heat for 30-40 minutes, stirring constantly.

3
Serve Warm

Top with honey or spiced butter for authentic flavor.

Cultural Significance

Marqaa symbolizes sustenance and patience in Oromo culture. Traditionally served to guests as a gesture of warmth, it’s also a staple during Meskel celebrations.
