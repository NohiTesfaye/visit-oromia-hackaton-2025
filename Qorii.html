Qorii

Oromo's Golden Butter Bread

About Qorii

Qorii is a rich, buttery flatbread made from wheat flour and spiced butter (Niter Kibbeh). It’s a celebratory dish, often served at weddings and festivals, symbolizing abundance and joy.

Making Qorii

Ingredients

  • Wheat flour
  • Spiced butter
  • Salt
  • Water

Traditional Preparation

1
Knead the Dough

Mix flour, water, and salt into a soft dough. Rest for 30 minutes.

2
Layer with Butter

Roll out dough, spread butter, fold, and repeat for flaky layers.

3
Cook on Griddle

Pan-fry until golden brown on both sides.

A Bread of Celebration

Qorii is central to Oromo weddings, where the bride and groom share it to symbolize unity. Its golden color represents prosperity in Oromo culture.
