Cumbo

The Soul of Oromo Cuisine

What is Cumbo?

Cumbo is a traditional Oromo dish made from roasted barley flour, mixed with spices and butter. It’s a staple food in the Oromo culture, often served during ceremonies and family gatherings. The rich, nutty flavor and hearty texture make it a beloved comfort food.

How to Prepare Cumbo

Ingredients

  • Roasted barley flour
  • Spiced butter (Niter Kibbeh)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)

Step-by-Step Recipe

1
Roast the Barley

Dry-roast barley grains until golden brown, then grind into fine flour.

2
Mix with Butter

Combine the flour with spiced butter and salt in a bowl.

3
Add Water Gradually

Knead the mixture while adding warm water until it forms a soft dough.

Cultural Significance

Cumbo is more than just food—it’s a symbol of Oromo identity and communal bonding. Traditionally served during Irreecha (thanksgiving festival) and weddings, it represents hospitality and unity.
